Belovezhskaya Pushcha National Park

Belovezhskaya Pushcha National Park was founded in 1932, but it was a preserve and not a national park. It became a national park in 1991. It is located within Kamyanyets District and Pruzhany District in Brest Region and Svislach District in Grodno Region.

Around 500 thousand tourists visit Belovezhskaya Pushcha every year. It’s main attractions are the excursions, museums, and the Residence of Father Frost. It was opened in 2003 and still serves a fantastic holiday spot for children to enjoy during winter.

The main attraction of the park are the animals and plants. For starters, you’ve of course got the European Bison. It is a very strong animal that is endangered. Belovezhskaya Pushcha is the only place where they live. There are also wolves, lynxes, otters and other animals. The plants there are also very interesting. There are many oaks that are more than 500 years old. There are also other plants, which include pines, rare grass plants, old ashes and silver firs.

It's a very memorable place to visit!
